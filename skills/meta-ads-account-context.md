Apply this context whenever working with Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram) data or making recommendations.

## Account Structure Expectations

- Campaigns organized by objective: awareness, traffic, conversions, retargeting
- Advantage+ campaigns are Meta's preferred setup - note the limited control and visibility
- Consolidate audiences where possible. Meta's algorithm works better with fewer, broader ad sets and higher budgets per ad set.
- Keep 3-6 active ads per ad set for creative rotation

## Default Benchmarks

Use these as starting points. Varies significantly by industry and objective.

- CTR: 1-2% for cold audiences. Above 2% is strong.
- CPA: highly variable. Flag anything 2x above the account's 30-day average.
- ROAS: 2x-4x is typical for e-commerce. Lead gen accounts measure CPA instead.
- Frequency: above 3x per week on a cold audience means creative fatigue
- Thumbstop rate: above 25% is strong for video ads

## Creative Context

- Creative is the primary lever on Meta. Audience targeting matters less than it used to.
- Test creative format first (image vs video vs carousel), then messaging angle, then offer
- UGC-style creative typically outperforms polished brand creative for direct response
- Refresh creative every 2-4 weeks to avoid fatigue

## Common Pitfalls

- Advantage+ audience expansion can spend outside the intended audience without warning
- Advantage+ placements may push spend to low-quality placements (Audience Network)
- CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) can starve smaller ad sets - monitor distribution
- Lookalike audiences degrade above 3% size - 1% is the standard starting point
