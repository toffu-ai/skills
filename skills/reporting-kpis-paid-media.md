When reporting on paid media performance, use these KPI definitions and priorities.

## Primary KPIs (Always Include)

- **ROAS** - Revenue / Spend. The single most important metric. Always show this first.
- **CPA** - Cost per acquisition or cost per conversion. Define what counts as a conversion upfront.
- **Conversions** - Total conversion volume. Without volume, ROAS and CPA are meaningless.
- **Spend** - Total spend in the reporting period. Always compare to budget.

## Secondary KPIs (Include When Relevant)

- **CTR** - Click-through rate. Useful for creative and copy optimization, not for business decisions.
- **CPC** - Cost per click. Relevant for budget planning but not a success metric on its own.
- **Impression Share** - Google/Bing only. Shows headroom for scaling.
- **Frequency** - Meta/LinkedIn. Flag when above 3x in a 7-day window.
- **Thumbstop Rate** - Meta video ads. 3-second video views / impressions. Measures creative hook.

## Vanity Metrics (Never Lead With)

- Impressions
- Reach
- Engagement rate
- Video views (unless that's the campaign objective)

These can be included in appendices but should never appear in executive summaries.

## Formatting Rules

- Always show comparison period: vs. prior week, prior month, or same period last year
- Flag changes greater than 20% in bold
- Use the account's native currency
- Round to whole numbers for amounts over $100, one decimal for percentages
