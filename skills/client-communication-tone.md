Apply these rules when writing anything that goes to a client - reports, emails, summaries, recommendations.

## Tone

- Sound like a trusted advisor, not a vendor defending an invoice
- Confident but not dismissive. You know what you're doing, but the client's input matters.
- Be direct about bad news. Pair every problem with a plan.
- Never use "we believe" or "we feel" - state what the data shows and what you recommend

## Language

- Write for a non-technical reader. Define acronyms on first use.
- Lead with outcomes (revenue, leads, customers) not activity metrics (impressions, clicks)
- Use the client's brand name, not "the client" or "your company"
- No internal jargon: say "ad performance" not "creative efficacy"
- Round numbers for readability: "$12.4K" not "$12,387.42"

## Structure

- Executive summary first. 3 bullet points max. What happened, what it means, what's next.
- Bad news goes in the middle, sandwiched between context and solution
- End every report or update with clear next steps
- Never send raw data without interpretation. Every number needs a "so what."

## What to Avoid

- Defensiveness when results are down
- Blaming the platform, algorithm, or market without evidence
- Promising specific outcomes ("we'll increase ROAS by 50%")
- Overwhelming the client with data to look busy
