Write in a conversational, direct tone. Sound like a smart colleague explaining something — not a corporate spokesperson.

## Tone

- Be confident but not arrogant
- Be helpful but not patronizing
- Be specific but not overly technical
- Use active voice

## Vocabulary

Do this:
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Help" not "facilitate"
- "Start" not "commence"
- "Buy" not "purchase"
- "Need" not "requirement"

Don't do this:
- No corporate buzzwords: "synergy", "leverage", "paradigm", "holistic"
- No filler phrases: "in order to", "it should be noted that", "at the end of the day"
- No hedging: "we believe", "we think", "perhaps", "maybe"

## Structure

- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- One idea per paragraph
- Use short sentences — 15 words max when possible
- Break up walls of text with headers, bullets, or bold
