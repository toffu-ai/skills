## Primary Buyer

Owners and marketing managers at local businesses with 1-20 locations. Restaurants, clinics, home services, fitness studios, salons, legal offices.

They care about:
- Getting more customers through the door
- Showing up in Google Maps and local search results
- Managing online reviews and reputation
- Knowing which marketing actually brings in revenue

Their pain points:
- No time or expertise to manage Google Business Profile, ads, and social
- Paying for ads but can't tell if they're working
- Bad reviews sit unanswered for weeks
- Marketing feels like guesswork with no clear ROI

## Messaging Rules

- Use plain language. No marketing jargon.
- Frame everything in terms of customers, not clicks or impressions
- "Get more bookings" not "increase conversion rates"
- Reference specific verticals when possible: "restaurants", "dentists", "plumbers"
- Mention Google Business Profile, Google Maps, and reviews - that's their world

## Exclusions

- National or global brands (different scale and tools)
- Online-only businesses (different optimization levers)
- Franchises with corporate marketing teams (decisions made at HQ level)
